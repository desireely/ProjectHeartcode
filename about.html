Types of Anxiety Disorders :

Phobias
Specific Phobia

Phobia of a specific object or situation

Symptoms of Specific Phobia:
  • Doing everything possible to avoid the object or situation
  • Feeling nauseated and anxious
  • Awareness that your fears are exaggerated but feeling powerless to control them
Social Phobia (Social Anxiety Disorder)

Profound fear of public humiliation and being singled out or judged by others in a social situation (caused by bullying, family conflict, sexual and emotional abuse)

Symptoms of Social Phobia:
  • Intense worry before, during, and after a social situation
  • Trying to blend into the background
  • Feeling a need to consume alcohol to help face a social situation
  • Missing school or work because of anxiety
Generalized Anxiety Disorder
Symptoms of Generalized Anxiety Disorder:
  • Restlessness
  • Feeling on edge or irritable
  • Being easily fatigued
  • Shortness of breath
  • Heart pilpitations
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Being easily fatigued
  • Muscle tension
  • Being easily fatigued
  • Headaches, muscle aches, stomachaches or unexplained pains
Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)
Types of Traumatic Events:
  • Combat exposure
  • Childhood physical abuse
  • Emotional abuse
  • Physical assault
Symptoms of PTSD:
  • Trouble sleeping
  • Flashbacks and severe anxiety
  • Easily startled or frightened
  • Trouble concentrating
  • Memory problems
